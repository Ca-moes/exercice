# Leg Workout — Research & Reasoning

Research behind the exercise selection in [[leg-workout]]. Consult when updating the routine or evaluating new exercises.

## Completeness Audit — Movement Pattern Map

| # | Muscle / Function | What it needs | Covered by | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Quads (compound) | Knee extension under load | Bulgarian split squats | ✅ Primary |
| 2 | Quads (isolation, stretch) | Deep knee flexion with quad stretch | Sissy squats | ✅ Primary |
| 3 | Hamstrings (knee flexion) | Curl pattern | Nordic curls | ✅ Primary |
| 4 | Hamstrings (hip extension) | Hinge pattern | Single-leg RDL | ✅ Primary |
| 5 | Glutes (extension) | Hip extension under load | Bulgarian split squats + Single-leg RDL | ✅ Primary |
| 6 | Glutes (abduction) | Lateral hip work | Side-lying leg raises / fire hydrants | ✅ Primary |
| 7 | Adductors | Lateral/medial hip work | Cossack squats | ✅ Primary |
| 8 | Calves (gastrocnemius) | Straight-knee calf raise | Single-leg calf raise off step | ✅ Primary |
| 9 | Calves (soleus) | Bent-knee calf raise | NOT covered | ⚠️ Optional |
| 10 | Hip flexors (strength) | Loaded hip flexion | NOT covered (but stretched in postural routine) | ⚠️ Optional |

**Verdict:** 8 out of 8 primary lower-body groups covered. 2 optional gaps (soleus and hip flexor strength) — neither is critical for your goals.

## Key Training Principle — Lengthened-Position Loading

Recent research (2022-2024) consistently shows that muscles grow more when loaded in their stretched (lengthened) position. This routine already applies this principle:

- **Nordic curls** — eccentrically loaded with hamstrings at full stretch. The gold standard.
- **Sissy squats** — loads rectus femoris in a deep stretch (mimics leg extension at long muscle length).
- **Single-leg calf raises off step** — the below-parallel stretch at the bottom is where the growth stimulus is highest.
- **Bulgarian split squats** — deep ROM at the bottom stretches hip flexors and loads quads at long muscle length.

Exercises where resistance peaks at the shortened position (e.g. band leg curls) are less effective for growth. This is why band leg curls rank last in our hamstring curl options.

## Exercise Rankings by Muscle Group

### Quads (Compound)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Pistol squat** | Very High | Full bodyweight on one leg through deep ROM. Requires significant strength + balance. The gold standard for bodyweight quad loading. |
| 2 | **Bulgarian split squat** | High | Single-leg loading makes bodyweight genuinely challenging. Deep stretch on the rear leg's hip flexor. Easier to learn than pistol squats. |
| 3 | **Step-ups (high box)** | Moderate-High | Good unilateral quad work. Limited by box height and balance. |
| 4 | **Bodyweight squat** | Low | Two-leg — too easy for anyone past beginner stage. Useful only as warmup. |

**Key insight:** The Bulgarian split squat is chosen over the pistol squat for Phase 1 because it's learnable in week 1 while still providing genuine single-leg challenge. Pistol squats require ankle mobility and balance that most people don't have initially.

### Quads (Isolation / Stretch-Focused)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Sissy squat** | High | Knees travel far forward, loading the rectus femoris in a deep stretch — mirrors the leg extension machine. 6-10 reps is plenty. |
| 2 | **Reverse Nordic curl** | High | Stronger by Science research: effective for quad strength and hypertrophy. Knee-dominant with significant quad stretch. |
| 3 | **Wall sit** | Low-Moderate | Isometric only. Good for endurance, limited for growth. |

### Hamstrings (Knee Flexion)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Nordic curl** | Very High | Multiple studies show 50-70% hamstring injury reduction. Eccentric emphasis creates more mechanical tension than concentric curls. A 5-second descent generates more stimulus than 10 fast reps. |
| 2 | **Sliding leg curl** (socks on hard floor) | Moderate | Lie on back, heels on sliders/socks, curl heels toward glutes. Good alternative if Nordics are too hard. |
| 3 | **Resistance band leg curl** | Low-Moderate | Ankle strap + band. Limited resistance curve. |

**Key insight:** Nordic curls are the most evidence-backed hamstring exercise in existence. Start with assisted negatives (use arms against a wall) and progress to full Nordics over months.

### Hamstrings (Hip Extension / Hinge)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Single-leg RDL** | High | Unilateral hip hinge — challenging for balance and posterior chain. Bodyweight alone provides decent stimulus because it's single-leg. |
| 2 | **Glute bridge (single-leg)** | Moderate | Good glute/hamstring activation but shorter ROM than RDL. |
| 3 | **Good morning (bodyweight)** | Low-Moderate | Bilateral — too light without load. Better with a barbell/dumbbells. |

### Calves

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Single-leg calf raise off step** | High | Full ROM (stretch below parallel, push to full contraction). Single-leg = challenging with bodyweight. |
| 2 | **Seated calf raise** (weight on knees) | Moderate | Bent-knee targets soleus (deeper calf). Add a dumbbell on the knees. Optional but improves completeness. |

## Progression Roadmap

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-8) — Foundation

| Exercise | Starting point | Goal by end of Phase 1 |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Nordic curls (assisted) | 3×5 (use arms to push back up) | 3×8 (less arm assistance) |
| Bulgarian split squats | 3×10/side | 3×15/side |
| Single-leg RDL | 3×10/side | 3×12/side with 2s pause at bottom |
| Sissy squats | 3×6 (hold doorframe) | 3×10 |
| Cossack squats | 2×10/side | 2×15/side |
| Side-lying leg raises | 2×20/side | 2×30/side |
| Single-leg calf raise | 4×12 | 4×15 |

### Phase 2 (Weeks 9-16) — Intensification

| Exercise | Upgrade |
|----------|---------|
| Nordic curls → **Full negatives** | Lower yourself in 5 seconds without arm assist |
| Bulgarian split squats → **Add pauses** | 2s hold at bottom of each rep |
| Single-leg RDL → **Add dumbbell** (3kg) | Small load, big balance challenge |
| Sissy squats → **Freestanding** | No doorframe. Hands on chest. |
| Cossack squats → **Deeper ROM** | Touch glute to heel on the working side |
| Leg raises → **Add band around ankles** | Resistance through full ROM |
| Calf raises → **Add slow eccentric** (3s down) | More time-under-tension |

### Phase 3 (Weeks 17-24) — Advanced

| Exercise | Upgrade |
|----------|---------|
| Nordic curls → **Full Nordic curls** (concentric + eccentric) | Lower AND pull yourself back up without hands |
| Bulgarian split squats → **Weighted** (backpack or dumbbells) | Add 5-10kg for progressive overload |
| Single-leg RDL → **Heavier load** (5-8kg) | Need heavier dumbbells |
| Sissy squats → **Weighted** (hold a dumbbell at chest) | Goblet-style hold adds quad load |
| Add: **Pistol squat progressions** | Start with assisted (hold doorframe), work toward freestanding |

### Phase 4 (Weeks 25+) — Equipment-Upgraded

| Exercise | With equipment |
|----------|---------------|
| Split squats → **Goblet squats** (heavy dumbbell) | 12-20kg — serious quad and glute loading |
| Single-leg RDL → **Dumbbell RDL** (bilateral, heavier) | 10-20kg per hand |
| Add: **Dumbbell lunges** (walking) | Loaded unilateral compound |
| Calf raises → **Weighted single-leg** | Hold dumbbell in one hand |

## Equipment Trigger Points

| Trigger | Equipment | Why now |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Bulgarian split squats at 3×15 with pause feel easy | **Backpack + weight** (free) | Lower body needs load to grow — bodyweight plateaus faster for legs than upper body |
| Single-leg RDL with 3kg × 12 easy | **Heavier dumbbells (8-12kg)** (~€20-30 used) | Hip hinge needs load for posterior chain development |
| Phase 3 overall | **Adjustable dumbbells (5-20kg)** | Legs are the strongest muscles — they outgrow bodyweight fastest. This is where equipment matters most. |

**Honest note:** Legs are the hardest muscle group to train with bodyweight alone. The muscles are simply too strong — you'll outgrow bodyweight exercises faster than for upper body. A pair of adjustable dumbbells will extend the useful life of your leg training by months.

## Revision — Week 3 (2026-06-14)

Now split across two short sessions/week (Legs 1 quad-focus + Legs 2 posterior-chain).

- **Nordic curls → Sliding leg curls.** The hamstring is biarticular (knee flexion + hip extension); Nordics train the eccentric **knee-flexion** pattern — but require an ankle anchor, which was the pain point (wedging under the treadmill). Sliding leg curls reproduce that same knee-flexion stimulus with **no anchor**, self-regulating via tempo. Banded lying curls are a backup. Sources: [e3rehab — Nordic curls](https://e3rehab.com/how-to-perform-nordic-hamstring-curls/), [sliding leg curl](https://bodybuilding-wizard.com/sliding-leg-curl-hamstring-exercise/).
- **Single-leg RDL → B-stance RDL.** Balance was the limiter, not the hamstrings — hence "I feel the stretch, not the muscle, and it's slow." The kickstand removes the balance demand and lets you actually load and drive the hinge. Sources: [Onnit — B-stance RDL](https://www.onnit.com/blogs/the-edge/how-to-do-b-stance-romanian-deadlifts-rdls-like-a-pro).
- **Hip thrust / glute bridge added** — direct glute work the old plan lacked; also supports the anterior-pelvic-tilt work.
- **Sissy squats and single-leg calf raises kept** (your favourites; calves now both days for frequency). Side-lying glute-med work demoted to an optional single focused set (rushed sets do nothing).
- **Right inner-groin:** Bulgarian split squats and Cossack squats load/stretch the adductors, so they're the likely aggravators — scale range or substitute bilateral work if it flares, pending the physio visit. The standard rehab path is isometric adductor squeezes (pain-free) progressing toward Copenhagen adduction. Informational, not a diagnosis. Sources: [e3rehab — adductor rehab](https://e3rehab.com/adductorstrainrehab/), [Physiotutors — Copenhagen](https://www.physiotutors.com/wiki/copenhagen-adduction-exercise/).

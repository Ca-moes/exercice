## Completeness Audit — Movement Pattern Map

| # | Muscle / Function | What it needs | Covered by | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lats (vertical pull) | Overhead pulling / lat stretch | Inverted rows (underhand) → pull-ups when bar available | ✅ Primary |
| 2 | Mid-back (rhomboids, mid traps) | Horizontal pulling + scapular retraction | Inverted rows (overhand) + prone Y-T-W | ✅ Primary |
| 3 | Lower traps | Scapular depression | Prone Y-raises | ✅ Primary |
| 4 | Rear delts | Horizontal abduction | Band pull-aparts | ✅ Primary |
| 5 | Upper traps | Shrugging | Band / dumbbell shrugs | ✅ Primary |
| 6 | Biceps — long head | Elbow flexion (arm at/behind body) | Inverted rows (supinated) + band curls | ✅ Primary |
| 7 | Biceps — short head | Elbow flexion (arm in front of body) | Concentration curls (dumbbell) | ✅ Primary |
| 8 | Forearms / grip | Grip endurance | All rowing and pulling (indirect) | ✅ Indirect |
| 9 | Rear rotator cuff | External rotation | Band pull-aparts (partial) + prone T-raises | ✅ Indirect |

**Verdict:** All 9 pull-relevant muscle groups/functions covered. The main limitation is lats — without a pull-up bar, vertical pulling is absent. This is the #1 equipment purchase for pull day.

## Exercise Rankings by Muscle Group

### Lats (Vertical Pull)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Pull-ups / chin-ups** (requires bar) | Very High | EMG shows highest lat activation of any back exercise. Nothing else comes close. |
| 2 | **Inverted rows (underhand/supinated grip)** | Moderate-High | PubMed study: latissimus dorsi showed very high EMG activation (>61% MVIC) during inverted rows. Supinated grip biases lats more than overhand. |
| 3 | **Band lat pulldowns** (standing, band over door) | Moderate | Simulates lat pulldown motion. Limited by band resistance but useful without a bar. |
| 4 | **Straight-arm pulldowns (band)** | Low-Moderate | Lat isolation without elbow flexion. Good for lat "connection" but lower overall activation. |

**Key insight:** Pull-ups are irreplaceable for lat development. Inverted rows are the best available substitute, but they're primarily a horizontal pull (mid-back emphasis). A pull-up bar is the #1 equipment priority for pull day.

### Mid-Back (Horizontal Pull)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Inverted rows (overhand/pronated grip)** | High | EMG: very high activation of mid-traps and rhomboids. Overhand grip shifts emphasis from lats to mid-back. Body angle controls difficulty. |
| 2 | **Band rows (seated)** | Moderate | Good scapular retraction work. Adjustable resistance via band thickness. |
| 3 | **Prone Y-T-W raises** | Moderate | EMG: greatest middle trap activation found with I-Y-T raises. Also hits lower traps and rear delts. Looks easy, burns intensely. |
| 4 | **Doorframe rows** | Low-Moderate | Limited ROM and awkward positioning but works in a pinch. |

### Rear Delts

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Band pull-aparts** | High | Direct rear delt isolation with constant tension. The closer the grip, the harder the contraction. |
| 2 | **Prone T-raises** (face-down, arms out to sides) | Moderate | Gravity-loaded rear delt work. Part of the Y-T-W complex. |
| 3 | **Wide-grip inverted rows** | Moderate | Rows with elbows flared hit rear delts more, but it's shared with mid-back. |

### Biceps

**Key principle — Lengthened-position training:** Recent research (2022-2024) shows that muscles grow more when loaded in a stretched (lengthened) position than when loaded in a shortened position. For biceps, this means exercises where the resistance is highest at the bottom (arm extended) are superior for hypertrophy. This is why incline curls and preacher curls outperform cable curls or band curls for growth — not because they're "harder" overall, but because the tension peaks where the muscle is most stretched.

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Resistance curve | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 1 | **Chin-ups** (requires bar) | Very High | Lengthened | Supinated grip + heavy load = top bicep builder. Hardest at the bottom (arms extended, biceps stretched). Also heavily loads lats. |
| 2 | **Incline dumbbell curls** | Moderate-High | Lengthened | The incline puts the shoulder in extension, stretching the biceps long head maximally. Tension is highest at the bottom. Jeff Nippard highlights this as one of his top 4 biceps exercises for stretch emphasis. Originally planned for Phase 3+, but moved to Phase 1 after the resistance band proved too strong and dumbbells were available. |
| 3 | **Concentration curls** (dumbbell) | Moderate | Lengthened-biased | Elbow braced on thigh prevents cheating. Gravity creates peak tension in the lower half of the curl (stretched position). Mimics preacher curl mechanics. |
| 4 | **Band curls** (anchored under foot) | Moderate | Shortened-biased | Constant tension but hardest at peak contraction (top). Resistance curve is backwards for optimal stretch-mediated growth. Replaced in Phase 1 due to band being too strong + inferior resistance curve. |
| 5 | **Doorway curls** | Low-Moderate | Variable | Grip doorframe, lean back, curl yourself forward. Effective but hard to progressively overload. |

**Session finding (Week 1):** The resistance band was too strong for curls, and stacking small dumbbells (2kg+3kg) created grip issues. However, this forced an early switch to incline dumbbell curls — which is actually a strict upgrade over band curls (better resistance curve). Any inclined surface works: treadmill seat, angled chair, pillows behind back on floor.

## The Pull-Up Bar Question

Without a pull-up bar, pull day has one significant limitation: **no vertical pulling**. Inverted rows are excellent for mid-back, but the lats need overhead pulling to be fully developed.

**Recommendation:** Buy a doorframe pull-up bar as your FIRST equipment purchase. It's €15-30 used and transforms pull day from "good" to "complete."

**Until you have one:** The routine works well for mid-back, rear delts, and biceps. Lats get partial work from supinated inverted rows and band pulldowns. This is enough for the first 4-8 weeks while you build the habit.

## Progression Roadmap

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-8) — Foundation (no pull-up bar)

| Exercise | Starting point | Goal by end of Phase 1 |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Inverted rows (table) | 3×8 at 45° angle | 3×12 at 45° angle |
| Prone Y-T-W raises | 3×8 each position | 3×12 each position |
| Band rows | 3×15 | 3×20 with slow squeeze |
| Band pull-aparts | 3×15 | 3×20 |
| Dumbbell shrugs | 3×15 (3kg) | 4×20 (3kg) |
| Band curls | 3×12 | 3×15 |
| Concentration curls | 3×12 (2-3kg) | 3×15 (3kg) |

### Phase 2 (Weeks 9-16) — Intensification + Pull-up bar

| Exercise | Upgrade |
|----------|---------|
| Inverted rows → **Lower angle** (more horizontal) | Progress toward fully horizontal body |
| Prone Y-T-W → **Hold water bottles / dumbbells** | Add 2-3kg to the raises |
| Band rows → **Thicker band + pause at contraction** | 2s squeeze at each rep |
| Buy **pull-up bar** → **Negative pull-ups** | Jump to top, lower yourself in 5 seconds. 3×5. |
| Band pull-aparts → **Slower tempo** (3s each way) | Increase time-under-tension |
| Band curls → **Thicker band** | More resistance |
| Concentration curls → **Slow eccentric** (4s down) | Increase intensity with current weight |

### Phase 3 (Weeks 17-24) — Advanced

| Exercise | Upgrade |
|----------|---------|
| Inverted rows → **Feet elevated rows** (feet on chair) | Significantly harder — nearly full bodyweight |
| Negative pull-ups → **Pull-ups** (full reps) | Start with 3×3-5, build to 3×8 |
| Prone raises → **Heavier load** (5-8kg) | Need heavier dumbbells |
| Band pull-aparts → **Face pulls** (band at face height) | More rotator cuff involvement |
| Band curls → **Chin-ups** (supinated pull-ups) | The best bicep exercise, period |
| Concentration curls → **Hammer curls** (heavier dumbbells) | Need 5-8kg dumbbells |

### Phase 4 (Weeks 25+) — Equipment-Upgraded

| Exercise | With equipment |
|----------|---------------|
| Pull-ups → **Weighted pull-ups** (backpack) | Add 5-10kg for progressive overload |
| Rows → **Dumbbell bent-over rows** | 10-20kg per hand — proper back loading |
| Raises → **Dumbbell reverse flyes** | Loaded rear delt isolation |
| Curls → **Dumbbell curls** (various grips) | Full bicep development with progressive overload |

## Equipment Trigger Points

| Trigger | Equipment | Why now |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Completed Phase 1 (or earlier if motivated) | **Pull-up bar** (€15-30) | Unlocks vertical pulling — the biggest gap in bodyweight pull training |
| Inverted rows at full horizontal × 12 feel easy | **Backpack + weight** (free) | Need external load for rows |
| Concentration curls at 3kg × 15 feel easy | **Heavier (adjustable) dumbbells** (5-8kg) | Biceps need load to grow |
| You're in Phase 4 | **Adjustable dumbbells (5-20kg)** | Unlocks bent-over rows, heavier curls, reverse flyes |

## Revision — Week 3 (2026-06-14)

Now split across two short sessions/week (Pull 1 + Pull 2).

- **Vertical pulling added (TS900 bar).** This was the single biggest gap — inverted rows can't replace vertical pulls. Progression: negatives / band-assisted → full pull-ups → weighted. Chin-ups (supinated) double as heavy biceps work later.
- **Inverted rows now on a fixed bar** instead of treadmill handles — grip and width are finally adjustable (overhand for back, underhand for lats/biceps).
- **Prone Y-T-W reduced to once/week** (Pull 2) — it's slow and the rows cover mid-back the rest of the week.
- **Biceps trained both days** (concentration on Pull 1, incline on Pull 2) to support arm growth. Curls use the 3 kg dumbbells; when 3×15 with a slow eccentric feels easy, that's the trigger for heavier (adjustable) dumbbells (the incline curl in particular wants proper per-hand loading).

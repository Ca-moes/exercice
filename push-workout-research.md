## Key Training Principle — Lengthened-Position Loading

Recent research (2022-2024) consistently shows that muscles grow more when loaded in their stretched (lengthened) position. This routine already applies this principle:

- **Wide push-ups with 3s eccentric** — slow descent maximizes time-under-tension at the bottom where chest is maximally stretched.
- **Deficit push-ups** (Phase 2) — extra ROM at the bottom = deeper stretch under load. This is why they're ranked #1 for chest.
- **Overhead band extension** — the overhead arm position ensures the long head is always somewhat stretched (biarticular: it crosses the shoulder). However, the band's resistance curve is backwards: easiest at the bottom (where the triceps are most stretched) and hardest at the top (where they're contracted). This is a known limitation — dumbbell overhead extensions (Phase 3+) fix this because gravity loads the stretched position correctly.
- **Dips** (Phase 3) — deep position stretches chest and triceps under heavy load.

The push exercises are mostly well-aligned with this principle because bodyweight pressing naturally loads the muscles hardest at the bottom of the movement (the stretched position). The one exception is the band extension — a necessary compromise in Phase 1 due to available equipment. The overhead position still makes it superior to pushdowns or kickbacks (which don't stretch the long head at all).

## Exercise Rankings by Muscle Group

### Chest (Horizontal Press)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Deficit push-ups** (hands on books/blocks) | High | EMG research shows deficit push-ups have the highest chest activation of any bodyweight press. The extra ROM at the bottom creates a deep stretch under load. |
| 2 | **Dips** (on chairs/parallettes) | High | EMG shows dips activate chest significantly when leaning forward. Also hits lower chest better than any push-up variation. |
| 3 | **Standard push-ups** | Moderate | ACE study: 61-63% chest activation vs bench press. Effective but plateaus fast without added load or leverage changes. |
| 4 | **Wide push-ups** | Moderate | Wider hand position shifts emphasis to chest stretch. Slower eccentric increases time-under-tension. |
| 5 | **Incline push-ups** (hands elevated) | Low | Reduced load. Good for beginners, warm-ups, or high-rep finishers. |

### Shoulders (Vertical Press)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Wall handstand push-ups** | Very High | Full bodyweight overhead. The gold standard for bodyweight shoulder pressing. Requires significant strength and balance. |
| 2 | **Pike push-ups (feet elevated)** | High | Feet on a chair shifts ~70% of bodyweight to shoulders. Steep angle mimics overhead press well. |
| 3 | **Pike push-ups (floor)** | Moderate | Good shoulder stimulus but less load than elevated version. Starting point for most people. |
| 4 | **Band/kettlebell lateral raises** | Moderate | Only way to isolate lateral delts. No bodyweight equivalent exists — you need bands or light weights. |

### Triceps (Isolation)

| Rank | Exercise | Intensity | Why |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | **Diamond push-ups** | High | ACE research: highest triceps activation of any bodyweight exercise. Narrow hand position forces triceps to dominate. |
| 2 | **Bodyweight skull crushers** (hands on chair edge) | High | Mimics gym skull crusher. Loads the long head in the stretched position. |
| 3 | **Overhead band extension** | Moderate | Targets long head (overhead position stretches it via shoulder flexion). Band resistance curve is backwards (easiest at stretch, hardest at contraction) — a compromise until dumbbells are available. Still better than kickbacks or pushdowns because the overhead position keeps the long head somewhat lengthened throughout. |
| 4 | **Bench/chair dips** | Moderate | Effective but shoulders take a lot of strain in the deep position. Watch for shoulder discomfort. |

## Progression Roadmap

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-8) — Foundation

Build the movement patterns, establish baseline.

| Exercise | Starting point | Goal by end of Phase 1 |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Push-ups | 3×10-12 | 3×20 with controlled tempo |
| Pike push-ups | 3×6-8 (floor) | 3×12 (floor) |
| Wide push-ups | 3×10 | 3×15 with 3s eccentric |
| Lateral raises | 3×15 (2kg kettlebells) | 3×20 (2kg) |
| Overhead band extension | 3×12 | 3×15 |
| Diamond push-ups | 2×6-8 | 2×12 |

### Phase 2 (Weeks 9-16) — Intensification

Harder variations, more load.

| Exercise | Upgrade |
|----------|---------|
| Push-ups → **Deficit push-ups** | Hands on books/yoga blocks for extra ROM |
| Pike push-ups → **Feet elevated pike push-ups** | Feet on chair, steeper angle |
| Wide push-ups → **Decline push-ups** | Feet elevated, targets upper chest |
| Lateral raises | 3kg kettlebells, or doubled band |
| Overhead extension | Thicker band or add slow eccentric (4s) |
| Diamond push-ups → **Bodyweight skull crushers** | Hands on chair edge, lower face toward hands |

### Phase 3 (Weeks 17-24) — Advanced

| Exercise | Upgrade |
|----------|---------|
| Deficit push-ups → **Weighted push-ups** (backpack) | Add 5-10kg in a backpack for progressive overload |
| Pike push-ups → **Wall handstand push-ups** | Full bodyweight overhead (start with partial ROM) |
| Decline push-ups → **Dips** (on chairs or parallettes) | Deeper ROM, higher load |
| Lateral raises | Heavier dumbbells if purchased (see equipment triggers) |
| Skull crushers | Add slow eccentric (4s down), pause at stretch |
| Diamond push-ups → **Close-grip deficit push-ups** | Diamond hand position on blocks |

### Phase 4 (Weeks 25+) — Equipment-Upgraded

| Exercise | With equipment |
|----------|---------------|
| Push-ups → **Dumbbell floor press** | Adjustable dumbbells — loaded horizontal pressing |
| Pike push-ups → **Dumbbell shoulder press** | Standing or seated — progressive overload with plates |
| Dips → **Weighted dips** (backpack/belt) | The best compound for chest + triceps with load |
| Lateral raises → **Heavier dumbbell lateral raises** | 5-10kg — proper overload for lateral delts |

## Equipment Trigger Points

| Trigger | Equipment | Why now |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| You can do 3×20 push-ups with 3s eccentric | **Backpack + weight** (free) | Need external load for progressive overload — push-ups alone have plateaued |
| Pike push-ups at 3×12 feel easy | **No equipment needed** — progress to wall handstand push-ups | |
| Lateral raises with 3kg feel easy at 20 reps | **Heavier dumbbells (5-8kg)** | Lateral delts ONLY respond to isolation — you need load |
| You want compound chest/tricep overload beyond push-ups | **Parallettes or dip station** (~€20-30 used) | Dips are the king of bodyweight upper push |
| You're in Phase 4 and want gym-equivalent loading | **Adjustable dumbbells (5-20kg)** (~€50-100 used) | Unlocks floor press, shoulder press, heavier lateral raises |

## Revision — Week 3 (2026-06-14)

Now split across two short sessions/week (Push 1 + Push 2), each muscle hit 2×/week.

- **Wide push-ups → Deficit push-ups (paused).** Wide push-ups are harder mainly due to a worse leverage/stability challenge, not better chest stimulus, and they were landing when already fatigued. Deficit push-ups (hands elevated ~10 cm, 1–2s pause) load the chest in the **lengthened position** — stretch-mediated hypertrophy — with controllable depth. Note: you don't need to *feel* an extreme stretch, just to load the muscle long. Sources: [Stronger by Science — stretch-mediated hypertrophy](https://www.strongerbyscience.com/stretch-mediated-hypertrophy/), [Nippard chest exercise ranking](https://barbend.com/news/jeff-nippard-ranks-chest-exercises-for-hypertrophy/).
- **Dips added (TS900).** A genuine chest + triceps compound, lengthened-position loading; forward lean = chest, upright = triceps.
- **Push-ups loaded, not just more reps.** 20 clean reps is endurance territory — progress via slow eccentric → deficit → loaded backpack rather than chasing 30+.
- **Lateral raises:** the lean-in variation is retained (loads the side delt at the bottom/stretched range). **Overhead triceps extension** moved to dumbbells (gravity loads the stretched long head correctly, unlike the band).
